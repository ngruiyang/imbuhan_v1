Awalan (prefixes)

PrefixFunctionExamples
meN- Active transitive or intransitive verb. Spelling changes depending on the root's first letter (see rules above). tulis → menulis (to write)
baca → membaca (to read)
goreng → menggoreng (to fry)
ber- Intransitive verb expressing a state, having something, or doing something without a direct object. Becomes be- before roots starting with r and a few fixed words. jalan → berjalan (to walk)
kerja → bekerja (to work)
rasa → berasa (to feel)
ter- Three uses: (1) accidental/unintentional action, (2) superlative, (3) passive state. jatuh → terjatuh (accidentally fell)
baik → terbaik (the best)
buka → terbuka (left open)
di- Passive voice — third-person agent. Never changes form. For first/second person use ku- or kau- instead. beli → dibeli (was bought)
makan → dimakan (was eaten)
tulis → ditulis (was written)
pe- Creates an agent noun (the person who does the action). Spelling mirrors meN- rules. tulis → penulis (writer)
nyanyi → penyanyi (singer)
baca → pembaca (reader)
ke- Forms ordinal numbers; also found in a small set of nouns and adjectives. tiga → ketiga (third)
empat → keempat (fourth)

Akhiran (suffixes)

SuffixFunctionExamples
-an Nominalises a verb or adjective. Can indicate the result of an action, a set, or an instrument. makan → makanan (food)
ajar → ajaran (teaching)
tanam → tanaman (plant/crop)
-kan Causative (cause something to happen) or benefactive (do something for someone). Commonly combined with meN-. buka → bukakan (open for someone)
bersih → bersihkan (to clean it)
tahu → maklumkan (to inform)
-i Repeated or directed action — implies the object is covered or repeatedly acted upon. Compare with -kan which focuses on result. tanda → tandai (to mark)
duduk → duduki (to occupy)
sayang → sayangi (to cherish)

Apitan (circumfixes)

CircumfixFunctionExamples
ke-…-an (1) Abstract noun expressing a quality or state. (2) Accidental passive — subject was caught in a situation. maju → kemajuan (progress)
baik → kebaikan (goodness)
hujan → kehujanan (caught in rain)
pe-…-an Place or process noun. Often denotes an institution, a field of practice, or an abstract process. belajar → pembelajaran (learning)
latih → pelatihan (training)
didik → pendidikan (education)
per-…-an Abstract noun or place where an action happens. dagang → perdagangan (trade)
main → permainan (game)
kawin → perkahwinan (marriage)
me-…-kan Active causative verb — the most common verbal circumfix in Malay. bersih → membersihkan (to clean)
besar → membesarkan (to enlarge)
tahu → memberitahukan (to inform)
me-…-i Active verb with a directed or covering sense — combines meN- with -i for transitive verbs. duduk → menduduki (to occupy)
sayang → menyayangi (to love)
ikut → mengikuti (to follow)
